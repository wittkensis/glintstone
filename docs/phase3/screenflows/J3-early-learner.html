What is Cuneiform? Lesson 1 of 5

Cuneiform is the world's oldest known writing system, invented in ancient Mesopotamia around 3400 BCE.
The name comes from Latin "cuneus" meaning "wedge" - because every mark is made by pressing a wedge-shaped reed into soft clay.

Signs, Not Letters

User Goal: Understand cuneiform is logographic, not alphabetic

J3-S4-signs
← Back to Curriculum
Lesson 3 of 5
SIGNS, NOT LETTERS
Unlike our alphabet, cuneiform signs can represent:
Alphabet:
A = one sound, always "ah"
Cuneiform:
AN = a word "god" OR a sound "an"
This means the same sign can be read differently depending on context - just like how "read" in English can be present tense (reed) or past tense (red).
