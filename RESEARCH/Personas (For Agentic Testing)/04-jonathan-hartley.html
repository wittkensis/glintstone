Primary Goals

  1. Accelerate digitization of backlogged collection

    130,000 tablets, budget for ~2,000/year. Any scalable solution that doesn't drain museum resources is valuable.

  2. Increase accessibility while protecting artifacts

    Researchers want more access. Digital alternatives can satisfy demand without physical handling risk.

  3. Demonstrate public value

    Needs to justify institutional funding. Visible impact metrics help make the case to leadership.

  4. Ensure proper attribution of BM materials

    British Museum must be credited as source. Institutional brand protection is non-negotiable.

Key Needs from the System

  • Collection integration

    Bulk import of museum metadata and images. Cannot manually enter 130,000 records.

  • Access controls

    Some tablets have publication restrictions. Must flag restricted materials and enforce compliance.

  • Institutional branding

    Clear acknowledgment of British Museum as source in all outputs.

  • Progress dashboards

    Show leadership how many tablets processed. Board presentations need compelling numbers.

  • Quality assurance

    Ensure outputs meet scholarly standards before public release. Museum reputation on the line.

Pain Points & Frustrations

  • Researcher demand exceeds capacity

    Scholars want immediate access to everything. Managing expectations is constant challenge.

  • Technology projects that overcommit

    Has seen partnerships that promised much but required heavy museum resources. Burned before.

  • Attribution disputes

    Researchers sometimes clash over credit. Platform must have clear provenance trails.

  • Legacy system integration

    Museum systems are old. Modern platforms need to work with existing infrastructure.

  • ROI pressure

    Every digitization investment must show return. Board wants metrics, not promises.
