### Problem Statement

Linguistic anthropology from the Mesopotamian region includes tens of thousands of existing physical artifacts that need to be transcribed, pieced together, translated, and fit into their proper contexts. There seems to be a deficit of people supporting the work needed, yet individuals like myself who see more and more of an opportunity to contribute alongside AI in helping breath life into these artifacts, and deepen our understanding of the past.

### Design Considerations

This solution needs to take into account the following:

1. The longstanding tradition of anthropology.
2. It needs a focus on informatics and respecting systems of navigability of existing knowledge, perhaps in a way that improves on what’s there today. 
3. It needs to give priority to academics’ final say on the accuracy of the outputs, allowing for refinement and approval from multiple academic authorities on various subjects.
4. It needs context and backtraceable, so that the original artifact with all of its contextual metadata can be viewed alongside any bottom-funnel outputs related to it.

### Hypotheses and Findings from Research

I've only been able to have one casual conversation on this, with a former archeologist over a beer. However, I have 3-4 Assyriology experts in mind I will reach out to following this to inform my Capstone project, and hope they respond.

While I'm waiting on their responses, I'll detail out what I've heard so far and what research questions I hope to gain more insight into.

**Participant 1 Insights**

1. Academic anthropology and archaeology are heavily influenced by the organizational politics of researchers themselves. A solution would need to take this into account, crediting researchers with contributions, giving weight to existing experts, and incorporating social networking considerations.
2. Some fragments may be missing for a long time, or never "found" as they're closely guarded by governments and authorities. For instance, the museums of Baghdad, Mosul, Damascus, Tehran, Bethlehem, etc, contain many artifacts that are not shared with the outside world due to current political strife in the region.
3. Historical context is extremely important to understanding the meanings during the translation process.
4. There may be things we will just never know, due to language being so organic and evolving, things getting lost to the sands of time. Similar to trying to decode what an animal is communicating -- how could we ever *validate* if we have the proper interpretation of what a dolphin or whale is saying to us?
5. There are varying degrees of confidence in interpretation, and these would need to be codified and accounted for.
