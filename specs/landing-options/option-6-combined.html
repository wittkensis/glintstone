What if there's a lost chapter of Gilgamesh in a museum drawer?

The Epic of Gilgamesh was pieced together from dozens of tablets found across different sites over more than a century. The most recent major fragment was discovered in 2015.

There could be more. We won't know until we look.

What observations did Babylonian astronomers record that we've never seen?

Ancient Mesopotamians tracked celestial events for centuries. Their records include observations of comets, eclipses, and planetary movements that modern astronomers use to calibrate historical chronologies.

Thousands of astronomical texts remain unread.

Whose letter home never got answered?

Among the tablets are personal letters: merchants writing to family, soldiers to loved ones, children to parents. Complaints, requests, expressions of worry.

These voices from the past are as human as anything written today.

How It Works

No ancient language skills needed. Just your eyes.

1

AI does the first pass

Machine learning identifies and suggests readings for individual signs.

2

You verify visually

Compare suggestions against tablet images. Pattern matching, not translation.

3

Consensus builds confidence

Multiple contributors verify each sign. Agreement creates reliability.

4

Scholars validate

Experts review transcriptions and unlock the meaning.

You Really Don't Need Expertise

You don't need to. You're doing visual pattern matching, not translation.

What if I make mistakes?

Everyone does. Each sign gets multiple readings, and statistical methods identify consensus.

How much time does it take?

As little as you want. One sign takes seconds. Every contribution helps.

How can my work be useful?

Crowd consensus is powerful. When many agree independently, that's strong evidence.
