How It Works

No ancient language skills needed. Just your eyes and attention.

1

View a tablet fragment

We show you a small section of a tablet image, focused on one or a few cuneiform signs.

2

Compare AI suggestions

Our AI proposes possible readings. You compare the shapes visually - like a matching game.

3

Vote on the best match

Select the option that looks most like what you see. Multiple contributors verify each sign.

4

Experts validate

Assyriologists review consensus transcriptions, ensuring accuracy while working more efficiently.

You really don't need expertise

I don't know any ancient languages

You don't need to. You're doing visual pattern matching, not translation. If a sign looks like a bird footprint, you can recognize that.

How can my work be useful?

Crowd consensus is powerful. When many people independently agree on what a sign looks like, that's strong evidence. Experts review the results.

What if I make mistakes?

Everyone does. The system is designed for it. Each sign gets multiple independent readings, and statistical methods identify reliable consensus.

How much time does it take?

As little or as much as you want. One sign takes seconds. Some people do five minutes a day, others spend hours. Every contribution helps.

The backlog problem

Museums hold over half a million cuneiform tablets - the earliest records of human civilization. Everything from royal decrees to shopping lists to love letters.

There are only about 200 people in the world actively transcribing them. At current rates, processing the full collection would take centuries.

Glintstone uses AI to do the initial heavy lifting, but human eyes are still essential for verification. That's where you come in.
