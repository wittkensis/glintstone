Background

Aisha is in year 4 of her PhD, working on Ur III administrative tablets from Nippur. She reads Sumerian competently and is developing Akkadian skills. She's digitally native, comfortable with computational tools, and sees AI as potentially transformative for her field.

Her dissertation involves transcribing ~200 tablets from Penn Museum storage, many unpublished. She's under time pressure to produce results and is always looking for ways to accelerate without sacrificing the quality that will define her career. Her advisor is skeptical of "digital shortcuts."

Primary Goals

  1. Accelerate dissertation transcription work

    Needs to transcribe 200 tablets. AI-generated first drafts she can correct would be transformative.

  2. Build professional reputation

    Job market is competitive. Needs visible contributions and publications before graduation.

  3. Learn from expert feedback

    Wants structured feedback on her transcriptions from senior scholars.

  4. Access tablets she can't physically examine

    Many relevant tablets are in museums she can't visit. High-quality images with AI assistance could expand her corpus.

Key Needs from the System

  • Workflow acceleration

    AI-generated first drafts she can correct, not blank canvases. Faster than doing it from scratch.

  • Learning scaffolding

    When expert reviewers modify her submissions, she needs to see why. Educational feedback.

  • Publication pathway

    How does Glintstone work fit into her dissertation? Can she cite it? Export formatted editions?

  • Attribution protection

    Clear credit for her contributions, even if AI-assisted. Her career depends on demonstrable expertise.

  • Parallel access

    Compare her readings with CDLI, Oracc, and expert suggestions. Multiple perspectives visible.

Pain Points & Frustrations

    Dissertation timeline is fixed. Every tool that doesn't accelerate her is wasted time.

    Many relevant tablets are in distant museums. Travel is expensive and time-consuming.

  • Career uncertainty about AI

    Unclear how hiring committees will view "AI-assisted" on her transcriptions.

  • Fear of errors damaging reputation

    Academic careers are built on accuracy. A published error could follow her for decades.

  • Advisor skepticism

    Her advisor is suspicious of "digital shortcuts." Needs to demonstrate rigor, not just speed.
