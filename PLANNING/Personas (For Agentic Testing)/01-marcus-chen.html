Background

Marcus has a BA in History with a focus on ancient civilizations but pivoted to software engineering for career stability. He browses r/cuneiform occasionally and has read popular books about Mesopotamia. He has no formal training in Assyriology and cannot read cuneiform, but finds the idea of "helping decode humanity's oldest writing" genuinely exciting.

His company gives him 20 hours of volunteer time per year. He's used Zooniverse before but found scrolling through blurry whale photos repetitive. He actively wants alternatives to doom-scrolling and would love something meaningful to do during lunch breaks or before bed.

Time Budget

Total hobby time 5-10 hrs/week
Available for platform 2-3 hrs/week

Primary Goals

  1. Contribute something meaningful during limited free time

    Wants to feel like he's making a real difference with his precious 15-minute windows, not just filling out surveys or doing make-work.

  2. Learn about ancient history without formal study commitment

    Interested in picking up knowledge incidentally through contribution, not through courses or textbooks he doesn't have time for.

  3. Escape doom-scrolling with something productive

    Actively looking for alternatives to social media that feel substantive rather than empty.

  4. Have something interesting to mention at dinner parties

    The "I help translate 4,000-year-old tablets" conversation starter has real social currency he values.

Key Needs from the System

  • Immediate value clarity

    Must understand what he's doing and why within 90 seconds of landing. No abstract descriptionsâ€”show him the actual work.

  • Task duration transparency

    Needs to know if a task fits his available time before starting. "This takes 3-5 minutes" is essential information.

  • Low cognitive load

    Visual tasks he can do without learning cuneiform. Pattern matching, comparison, not linguistic analysis.

  • Progress visibility

    Concrete evidence his contributions matter. Numbers going up, tablets attributed, impact made visible.

  • Mobile-first design

    Does most hobby activities on his phone. Must work well on iPhone during commute or in waiting rooms.

Pain Points & Frustrations

  • Intimidated by academic jargon

    Terms like "logogram," "determinative," and "Neo-Babylonian period" without explanation make him feel like he doesn't belong.

  • Fear of "messing up"

    Anxiety about making mistakes that waste expert time or corrupt important research.

  • Uncertain value of contributions

    If he can't see that his work actually helps, he won't return. Needs the feedback loop to be tight and visible.

  • Long tutorials before action

    If he has to watch a 10-minute video or read multiple screens before contributing, he'll bookmark the site and never return.

  • Lack of clear stopping points

    Needs to know when he's at a good place to pause. Task flows that don't signal completion create anxiety.
