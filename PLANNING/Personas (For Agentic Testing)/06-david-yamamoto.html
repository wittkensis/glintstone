Background

David teaches undergraduate courses on Ancient Mesopotamia and graduate seminars on cuneiform sources. He's not an Assyriologist by training (his PhD is in Ancient History), but he uses cuneiform sources in translation regularly.

He's always looking for ways to make 4,000-year-old material feel relevant to students raised on smartphones. Hands-on activities work better than lectures, but designing good primary source exercises is time-consuming.

Time Budget

Course prep 5-10 hrs/week (semester)
Class sessions 75-minute blocks
Student project oversight Variable

Primary Goals

  1. Give students hands-on experience with primary sources

    Reading about tablets isn't the same as engaging with them. Wants students to feel like historians.

  2. Demonstrate digital humanities in practice

    Wants students to understand how technology is changing historical research. Real example better than theory.

  3. Create engaging assignments without cuneiform literacy

    His students can't read cuneiform. Needs activities that are meaningful without linguistic expertise.

Key Needs from the System

  • Classroom mode

    Guided activities suitable for groups. Students should be able to work simultaneously without interference.

  • Assignment templates

    Pre-built exercises with clear learning objectives. Reduces his prep time significantly.

  • Student progress tracking

    Needs to see what students have contributed for grading and engagement assessment.

  • Scholarly accuracy

    Can't teach wrong information. Platform content must meet academic standards.

Pain Points & Frustrations

  • Limited prep time

    Designing custom activities takes hours. Needs ready-to-use materials.

  • Student tech issues

    Class time wasted on login problems, browser issues, or confusing interfaces.

  • Variable student engagement

    Some students love hands-on work; others just want to check boxes. Needs to track both.

  • Integration with LMS

    Canvas, Blackboardâ€”students live there. Anything outside is extra friction.
